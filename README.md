###  About Me

I studied sociology because I became convinced early on that the entanglement of data, algorithms, and political life is one of the defining problems of our time — and that addressing it requires both computational power and theoretical depth.

Since then I've been building a bridge between social theory — Arendt, Weber, Habermas, Rawls — and computational methods — NLP, causal inference, multi-agent simulation. I care about how algorithmic systems interact with material political-economic structures, and whether the emergent dynamics of social life can be made legible, or even predictable.

Something between *Person of Interest* and Laplace's Demon.
